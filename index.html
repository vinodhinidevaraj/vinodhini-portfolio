Understanding Customer Satisfaction Through Data

Published on March 2026 • 8 min read

In this analysis, I explored the Amazon Customer Behavior Survey dataset to understand what truly drives customer satisfaction. The dataset consisted of 602 observations covering purchase habits, browsing frequency, ratings, and demographic information.

Exploring Purchase & Browsing Patterns

The majority of customers reported purchasing a few times a month, while browsing behavior showed higher weekly engagement. These initial descriptive statistics gave a foundational view of customer activity patterns.

Is The Data Normally Distributed?

Using histogram visualization and the Anderson-Darling test, I examined whether the dataset followed a normal distribution. The results clearly indicated non-normality across multiple significance levels. This meant parametric tests were not ideal for further statistical modeling.

Correlation Insights

One of the strongest findings was the relationship between Rating Accuracy and Shopping Satisfaction, with a correlation coefficient of 0.514 — indicating a moderate positive relationship. Interestingly, age showed almost no correlation with satisfaction.

Regression Model

A simple linear regression model revealed that Rating Accuracy explains approximately 26.4% of the variance in Shopping Satisfaction (R² = 0.264). The coefficient was statistically significant (p < 0.001), reinforcing that accurate ratings significantly impact customer experience.

Customer Segmentation

I implemented K-means clustering using purchase frequency, review importance, and satisfaction scores to identify behavioral segments. This segmentation can help businesses tailor marketing strategies for different customer groups.

Key Takeaway

Customer satisfaction is strongly influenced by trust in ratings and reviews. Improving rating transparency and personalization mechanisms can significantly enhance user experience in e-commerce platforms.
